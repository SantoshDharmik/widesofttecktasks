History of the Internet

History of the Internet from 1960 to 2025

1960s: The Beginning (Concept & Research)
1965: First network experiment between two computers by Lawrence Roberts.
1969: ARPANET started by the U.S. Department of Defense, connecting 4 universities. It was the first version of the Internet.
1970s: Growth of Networking
1971: First email sent by Ray Tomlinson.
1973: First international ARPANET connection (UK and Norway).
1974: The word “Internet” was used in a paper by Vinton Cerf and Bob Kahn (they are known as the "fathers of the Internet").
1978: First spam email sent.
1980s: Internet Protocols & Expansion
1983: TCP/IP protocol adopted, forming the modern Internet.
1985: First domain name registered: symbolics.com.
1989: World Wide Web (WWW) proposed by Tim Berners-Lee.
1990s: The Internet Goes Public
1991: WWW made public; first website created.
1993: First web browser Mosaic launched (made web easy to use).
1995: Big companies start using the Internet (like Amazon, Yahoo, eBay).
1998: Google founded.
2000s: Rise of Social Media & Mobile Internet
2004: Facebook launched.
2005: YouTube launched.
2007: First iPhone released – mobile Internet boom starts.
2008: Internet users crossed 1 billion globally.
2010s: Smart Era & Streaming Boom
2010: Instagram launched; rise of image/video sharing.
2016: TikTok (as Douyin) launched.
2018: Voice search and AI assistants (like Alexa, Siri) become popular.
2019: 5G network rollout begins in some countries.
2020–2025: AI & Internet of Everything
2020: COVID-19 increases dependence on the Internet (online work, classes, meetings).
2023: AI tools (like ChatGPT) become popular.
2024–2025: Internet used in smart homes, AI bots, metaverse, wearables, driverless cars, and more.
Over 5 billion users are now online.
